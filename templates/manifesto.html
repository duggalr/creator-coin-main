"Manifesto"

Here's to all the builders. 🥂

There seems to be a bit of discrepancy in the world. Many hackers spend their weekends and any ounce of free-time they get, tinkering away and building new stuff. If you think about all the great innovations over the past century, they usually originated from there. However, the sad reality is, there really isn't any optimal solution to help fund this early work. Thus, hackers go to their dreaded 'day-job', to help make ends meet.

There are donation platforms, like Patreon, which serve as the dominant medium to help fund individuals side projects. People support the creator's work, by subscribing to a monthly donation. The platform is great, and provides immense value to others. However, like many donation-based models, you end up with the "free-rider problem". Only a small % of people actually donate. And the backers don't have have any real monetary incentive.

Let's take Wikipedia. I am almost certain everyone viewing this page has visited or fetched knowledge from Wikipedia over the past month. However, how many have actually donated to Wikipedia? And what % of those donate on a monthly basis? Wikipedia provides an immense value to society, but captures very little monetary value for itself.

Another example would be the early researchers and scientists involved in creating the foundational protocols (ie. TCP/IP) of the Internet. Their innovations produced immense value to society, yet very little monetary value captured for themselves.

I quickly put together this site (hence the ultra-basic UI...), to allow hackers to publish a NFT of themselves. Similar to Hackaday, you can share updates on the project(s) you are working, and add your github. To help fund your work, you can easily launch a NFT for yourself. People who like your work, can buy it.

The NFT serves as an 'investment', into the creator. Here, the backer can support a creator they like, while at the same time receiving a NFT, which can potentially be worth quite a bit some day... The dynamics here are a bit different than Pateron.

The creator can enable many exclusive benefits for supporters owning the NFT. Who knows, if the creator's project is successful, early NFT owners may even get exclusive token drops? Either way, there is complete freedom for how the creator would want to structure his benefits. If the creator's project fails 😑, well, I'm sure they will have many others!

To start, I have created a NFT for myself! Here's my profile. If you like the project(s) I am working on, consider buying my NFT!

Got any questions you want to ask me? Reach out! By the way, all the code for the site is open-source, so feel free to contribute!
